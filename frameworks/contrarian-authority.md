# Contrarian Authority Framework

Challenge a widely-held belief in your industry, then show the deeper principle most people miss.

---

## Structure Template

```
1. Contrarian Hook — "Most [audience] think [conventional wisdom], but [contrarian insight]"
2. Authority Stacking — 2-3 industry truisms that set the scene
3. Single Unifying Principle — the real driver behind the surface-level observation
4. Systematic Breakdown — 4-6 numbered strategies with brief tactical guidance
5. CTA — direct engagement request tied to the post's core insight
```

---

## When to Use

- You hold a view that cuts against the common advice in your field
- You've seen clients or teams fail at something "everyone" recommends
- You want to establish deep expertise, not just surface familiarity
- You're introducing a framework or methodology for the first time

---

## Example

**Hook:**
Most project managers think adding more process reduces chaos. But the teams I've seen thrive have less process, not more — and a clearer principle instead.

**Authority stacking:**
Project management tools are everywhere. Certification programs keep growing. Yet delivery timelines keep slipping.

**Unifying principle:**
The real leverage isn't documentation — it's shared decision-making criteria. When everyone knows *how* to decide, the process takes care of itself.

**Breakdown:**
1. Define the 3 questions every decision should answer
2. Replace status meetings with async decision logs
3. Cut approval chains by one layer — see what breaks
4. Measure outcomes, not activity
5. Revisit the criteria quarterly, not the process docs

**CTA:**
What's one process your team follows that nobody questions? Drop it below — I'll tell you whether it's load-bearing or just habit.

---

## Tips

- The contrarian hook works best when it's genuinely surprising but defensible — not just inflammatory
- Authority stacking should name real trends or tools your audience recognises, not vague generalities
- Each numbered point should contain one concrete action, not a paragraph of explanation
