# Instead Pattern Framework

Reframe a common habit or approach. Show the old way, offer the better way, and explain why it works.

---

## Structure Template

```
1. Authority Hook — "If I had to [do X] from scratch today, here's how I'd approach it:"
2. 4-6 Instead Blocks — each one follows the same structure:
   - Stop [old behaviour]
   - Start [new behaviour]
   - Why it works: [one-sentence reason]
3. CTA — invite readers to share which shift resonates or claim a resource
```

**Each Instead Block:**
```
Stop [conventional approach].
Start [better approach].
Why it works: [strategic reasoning in one sentence].
```

---

## When to Use

- You want to rehabilitate a topic that's full of outdated advice
- You have strong opinions built from real experience
- Your audience is sophisticated enough to appreciate the nuance of "old vs new"
- You want a post that's easy to skim but rewards careful reading

---

## Example

**Hook:**
If I had to build a client reporting process from scratch today, I'd throw out most of what I was taught. Here's the approach I'd actually use:

**Instead blocks:**

Stop sending weekly status emails.
Start publishing a live dashboard with async comment threads.
Why it works: status emails answer the wrong question — "what happened?" instead of "what do we decide next?"

Stop measuring hours delivered.
Start measuring decisions unblocked.
Why it works: clients don't care about effort, they care about momentum.

Stop writing reports for the person who hired you.
Start writing them for the person who reads them at 6am before a board meeting.
Why it works: format follows audience, not relationship.

Stop waiting for the monthly review to flag problems.
Start building a single-metric early warning signal.
Why it works: problems caught in week two cost a fraction of problems caught in month two.

**CTA:**
Which of these would change the most for your team right now? Drop a number below.

---

## Tips

- The hook requires some credibility to land — "if I had to build X from scratch" implies you've built it before
- Each "why it works" line should explain the mechanism, not just restate the benefit
- 4-6 instead blocks is the sweet spot — fewer feels underdeveloped, more becomes exhausting
