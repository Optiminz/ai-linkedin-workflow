# Guide 06: Content Strategy

**Why this matters:** One great post doesn't build an audience. Consistent, recognizable content does.

---

## Why strategy beats individual brilliance

You could write the best post of your career and have it go nowhere. You could write an average post that lands perfectly because it's the fifth thing someone's seen from you this month and they're finally paying attention.

Consistency compounds. People need to see a name multiple times before they follow it, let alone reach out. Strategy is what makes consistency possible without burning out.

---

## What content pillars are

Pillars are the 2–3 topics you're known for. Not everything you could write about — what people will come to you for.

The narrower you go, the faster recognition builds. "I write about leadership" competes with everyone. "I write about why finance leaders can't get buy-in for system changes" is a position.

---

## How to find yours

Look at your last 20 posts, if you have them. What topics repeat? What got the best responses — not just likes, but comments from the right people, DMs, people sharing it with someone specific?

If you're starting fresh, ask: what do people already come to me for? What questions do I get over coffee that I enjoy answering? What frustrates me in my field that most people seem to accept?

Three or fewer. More than three and nothing becomes a signal.

---

## The /strategy skill

When you're ready to work through pillars in a structured way, run `/strategy` in a Claude Code session. It'll walk you through a guided conversation — your background, your audience, what you're trying to build — and help you draft a content strategy document saved to `projects/linkedin/`.

You don't need it to start. But it's there when you want more structure than this guide provides.

---

## Cadence: be honest with yourself

- **1 post per week:** Solid. Builds slowly but sustainably. This is where most people should start.
- **3 posts per week:** Aggressive growth mode. Only works if you have a backlog or a system that makes drafting fast.
- **5+ posts per week:** Requires significant infrastructure. Not recommended without a tested workflow.

The number that matters is the one you can hit for six months without it feeling like a grind. Start there. Increase only after it feels routine.

---

## Post types

**Thought Leadership**
Perspective and expertise. Your take on something happening in your field. Disagreements with received wisdom. Frameworks for how to think about a problem. These build authority.

**Conversion**
Designed to generate inbound. Addresses a specific pain, demonstrates you solve it, includes a soft signal for next steps. These generate leads.

**Practitioner**
Sharing real work. A process you use. A tool you built. A mistake made and what you learned. These build credibility and relatability simultaneously.

**Feel Good**
Personal stories, wins worth celebrating, human moments. These build connection. Don't overdo them — they work because they're occasional.

---

## Suggested mix

A starting point, not a rule:

| Post Type | Share |
|---|---|
| Thought Leadership | 40% |
| Conversion | 30% |
| Practitioner | 20% |
| Feel Good | 10% |

If your goal is primarily lead generation, weight toward Conversion. If you're building a reputation in a new field, weight toward Thought Leadership. Adjust based on what's actually working — the post-log in `projects/linkedin/post-log.md` is for tracking this.
