## 1. Purpose

AI writing tools default to a recognizable style: smooth, formal, slightly breathless, and full of the same phrases. Over time, your content starts to sound like everyone else's.

This document prevents that. It tells the AI:
- What words and phrases to avoid (AI Tells)
- What tone and intensity to aim for (Voice Dimensions)
- What patterns are distinctly yours to protect (Keep List)
- How to format content for LinkedIn specifically

Every agent or prompt in this workflow reads this document before generating anything. If something sounds off, come back here and add a rule.

## 3. AI Tells — Prohibited Words and Phrases

These are words and phrases that signal "written by AI" to anyone who reads a lot of LinkedIn. They're not wrong, exactly — they're just overused to the point of meaninglessness, and they tend to cluster together in AI-generated content.

Remove these from all drafts. Add your own as you notice them.

### 3.1 The Classic Overused Verbs
- **delve** — Use: look at, explore, dig into
- **leverage** — Use: use, apply, take advantage of
- **unlock** — Use: open up, enable, reveal
- **harness** — Use: use, tap into
- **spearhead** — Use: lead, drive, run
- **streamline** — Use: simplify, speed up, cut steps from
- **supercharge** — Use: speed up, improve, strengthen

### 3.2 Adjectives That Mean Nothing
- **seamless** — If it matters, explain why it's smooth
- **robust** — Use: reliable, thorough, well-built, or just describe it
- **cutting-edge** — Use the actual thing that makes it current
- **dynamic** — Vague. Say what actually changes or adapts
- **innovative** — Vague. Describe the actual innovation
- **comprehensive** — Vague. Say what's actually covered
- **holistic** — Vague. Say what the whole thing includes

### 3.3 Hype Phrases
- **game-changing** — Use: significant, important, useful, or describe the change
- **revolutionary** — Use: new approach, different method, or describe what changed
- **transformative** — Use: meaningful change, material difference, or describe what shifted
- **disruptive** — Overused. Say what it actually changes
- **groundbreaking** — Overused. Say what's actually new
- **paradigm shift** — Avoid. Describe the actual shift
- **next-level** — Avoid. Say what level you're actually at

### 3.4 Hollow Transitional Phrases
- **"Here's the kicker"** — Just say the thing
- **"Here's what nobody tells you"** — If it's true, prove it
- **"Here's the thing"** — Cut it, say the thing directly
- **"Let's dive in"** — Just start
- **"Without further ado"** — Cut entirely
- **"That said..."** — Often just filler before the real point
- **"At the end of the day"** — Vague. Say what actually matters
- **"In today's world"** — Vague opener. Start with the actual point
- **"It goes without saying"** — Then don't say it
- **"Needless to say"** — Same

### 3.5 False Modesty and Filler
- **"I'm not an expert, but..."** — If you're posting, you have a perspective. Own it.
- **"Take this with a grain of salt"** — If you're not confident, don't post it yet
- **"Just my two cents"** — Undercuts your point before you make it
- **"This might be controversial, but..."** — Say the thing

### 3.6 Padding That Adds Words, Not Meaning
- **"In order to"** — Use: to
- **"Due to the fact that"** — Use: because
- **"At this point in time"** — Use: now
- **"In the event that"** — Use: if
- **"Utilize"** — Use: use
- **"Facilitate"** — Use: help, allow, enable (whichever is accurate)
- **"Demonstrate"** — Use: show

### 3.7 Closing Cliches
- **"What are your thoughts?"** — Generic. Ask something specific.
- **"Drop a comment below"** — Obvious. Either ask a real question or don't ask.
- **"Let me know in the comments"** — Same problem.
- **"Tag someone who needs to see this"** — Feels like engagement bait now.
- **"Share this with your network"** — Ask only when it genuinely fits.

**Example patterns to inspire yours:**

- **Honest progress framing** — Saying "working towards" or "still figuring out" instead of claiming mastery you don't have. This builds more trust than false confidence.
  - Example: "We're still figuring out how to make this sustainable" not "We've cracked the code on sustainability"

- **Specific attribution** — Naming the actual tool, company, person, or framework. Not "a popular automation platform" — Zapier. Not "a leading researcher" — name them.
  - Example: "We use Notion, not just 'a project management tool'"

- **Approximate language** — Using "roughly" and "around" for estimates instead of false precision.
  - Example: "Around 80% of the time" not "80.3% of cases"

- **Real constraint acknowledgment** — Referencing actual limits of time, budget, or team size. Makes advice land better.
  - Example: "Without hiring a full team" or "Given we're a team of four"

- **Colorful industry terms** — Specific vocabulary that signals insider understanding. Use your own field's language naturally.
  - Example: "cowboy coding", "duct-tape solution", "technical debt"

- **Outcome explanations** — Always saying WHY something matters after stating WHAT it is. Readers don't want to work to understand the point.
  - Example: "This reduces manual steps — which matters because your team's time is the constraint, not the software"

## 5. Formatting Rules

These rules are specific to LinkedIn. LinkedIn does not render markdown formatting — asterisks and underscores appear as literal characters, not bold or italic.

### 5.1 Text Formatting
- No `**bold**` or `_italic_` markup — it will appear as literal asterisks
- Use ALL CAPS sparingly for genuine emphasis (one word maximum)
- Short paragraphs: 2-3 sentences maximum
- White space is your friend — blank lines between thoughts
- Lists: use • character, not markdown `-` or `*`

### 5.2 Emoji Use
- 2-4 emojis maximum per post
- Place at start of lines or after key points, not mid-sentence
- Use for visual scannability, not decoration
- Consistency matters — if you use them, use them every post

### 5.3 Hook (First Line)
- This is the only line visible before "see more" on mobile
- It must create enough curiosity to make someone tap
- No "I'm thrilled to share..." or "Excited to announce..."
- Ask a question, make a surprising statement, or start mid-thought

### 5.4 Engagement Questions
Make them specific and low-barrier. Generic questions get ignored.

Good patterns:
- "Anyone else notice..." — invites agreement, low pressure
- "Curious what others have found with..." — genuine question, not fishing
- "For anyone working through something similar..." — inclusive, not exclusive
- "What's your take on..." — when you actually want to know

Avoid:
- "What are your thoughts?" — too generic
- "Am I the only one?" — rhetorical, feels like fishing

### 5.5 Post Length
- Standard post: 150-300 words
- Long-form: 300-500 words (use sparingly, needs a strong hook)
- Never pad to hit a word count — cut instead

## 6. CTA Patterns

Not every post needs a call to action. When you use one, match the ask to the value you've offered.

### 6.1 Soft (Low Ask)
For posts where you've shared something useful and want to continue the conversation:
- "Drop me a message if you're working through something similar"
- "Happy to share the template — just message me"
- "If this is relevant to what you're building, let's connect"

### 6.2 Medium (Specific Exchange)
For posts with a clear resource or follow-up:
- "Comment [keyword] and I'll send you..."
- "Reply here if you want the full breakdown"
- "Message me 'RESOURCE' and I'll send the link"

### 6.3 Direct (External)
For posts where you're driving somewhere specific:
- "Link in comments" (put the link in first comment, not the post)
- "Full article linked below"
- "Book a call — link in bio"

### 6.4 CTA Rules
- One CTA per post maximum
- Match the ask to the value offered — don't ask for a lot after giving a little
- Never use a CTA on a personal story or vulnerable post — it undermines the moment
- Avoid "like and share if this helped" — it reads as engagement bait

## 7. Quality Check

Before any post goes out, run through this list:

- [ ] No AI Tells from Section 3
- [ ] Voice matches your Voice Dimensions from Section 2
- [ ] First line works as a hook without the rest of the post
- [ ] No markdown formatting symbols visible (`**`, `__`, `-` for lists)
- [ ] Emoji count: 2-4
- [ ] One clear point — not three good ideas crammed together
- [ ] CTA (if present) matches the value of the post
- [ ] Would you say this out loud? If not, rewrite it.
