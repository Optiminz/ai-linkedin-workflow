# Hook Bank

Reusable hooks organized by type. Add new ones as you develop posts.

## How to Use

- `/lidev` reads this file and may suggest stored hooks
- After developing a post, unused hook options get saved here
- Steal hooks from posts you admire — break down why they work
- Before writing a new hook from scratch, check here first

---

## Contrarian Hooks

Flip a common assumption. The reader should think "wait, that's not how I thought about it."

| Hook | Notes |
|------|-------|
| "Automating a broken process doesn't fix it. It just breaks it faster." | Sharp one-liner. Works as a standalone hook or mid-post payoff. Pairs well with any AI readiness or process design post. |
| "You don't need more staff. You need better systems. But nobody wants to hear that when they're drowning." | Contrarian but empathetic — the second sentence earns the trust back after the provocation. Good for capacity/operations posts. |
| "The most expensive tech decision most teams make isn't buying the wrong tool. It's waiting too long to commit to the right one." | Flips the usual "don't rush into tech" advice. Works for tool adoption, AI investment, or digital transformation angles. |
| "Everyone's talking about which AI tool to use. Nobody's talking about whether their operation is ready for one." | Classic contrarian structure: what everyone does vs. what actually matters. Strong for AI readiness, change management posts. |

## Story Hooks

Drop the reader into a scene or moment. Something already happened — they need to know what.

| Hook | Notes |
|------|-------|
| "A $70K decision was about to be made on guesswork. We proposed spending $16K to find out if it was the right decision at all." | Concrete numbers do the heavy lifting. The word "guesswork" creates instant tension. Works for diagnostic-first, pre-investment angles. |
| "Two years building a system. It only started actually working two weeks ago." | Aspirational honesty — implies the journey was harder than it looked. Strong for practitioner/build posts where you share what finally clicked. |
| "You've asked AI to handle something complex at work. It hallucinated, missed half the fields, or gave you something you'd never send to a client. So you rewrote the prompt. Same result." | Puts the reader inside a frustrating experience they've almost certainly had. High empathy entry point for AI workflow posts. |

## Question Hooks

A good question hook creates a gap the reader needs to close. Weak version: "Have you ever wondered...". Strong version: a question that implies something is wrong.

| Hook | Notes |
|------|-------|
| "How much of your week is spent hunting for things that already exist somewhere in your systems?" | Simple, universal pain point. Works for knowledge management, systems design, or digital blindspot posts. |
| "Before you sign: do you know what problem you're actually solving?" | Puts the reader at peak decision pressure — mid-process, budget approved, vendor demo done. High relevance for any pre-investment post. |

## Pattern Interrupt Hooks

Disrupt the scroll with an unexpected image, analogy, or frame. Should make the reader do a double-take.

| Hook | Notes |
|------|-------|
| "The riskiest person in your firm isn't who you think. It's the one everyone goes to when they don't know how something works." | Opens with a puzzle before the insight. Creates genuine curiosity — the "riskiest person" frame is counterintuitive. Best for knowledge management, single-point-of-failure posts. |
| "AI has the knowledge of 200 PhDs and the wisdom of a 3-year-old. That's not a flaw. That's the design." | Extremely sticky image. The second sentence earns it — reframes limitation as design constraint. Strong for AI adoption, human-AI partnership posts. |
| "DNS record panels are like car radios. They all do the same thing but every brand puts the buttons somewhere different, so nobody ever truly learns how to use them." | Unexpected analogy that reframes a shared frustration. Best used as a mid-post payoff rather than the opening — burns the best line too early if used as a hook. |
